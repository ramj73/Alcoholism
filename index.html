🎯 Key Research Targets

Biological Targets

Five key biological systems involved in Alcohol Use Disorder. Click a card to explore its genes, or click a gene tag to view its protein structure.

⚡

Dopamine Reward System

Affects reward sensitivity and addiction vulnerability. The dopamine system is central to how the brain processes pleasure and motivation.

DRD2 DRD4 DAT1
DRD2 — D2 Dopamine Receptor
Mediates reward signaling; reduced expression linked to addiction risk.
DRD4 — D4 Dopamine Receptor
Associated with novelty-seeking behavior and impulsivity.
DAT1 — Dopamine Transporter (SLC6A3)
Regulates dopamine reuptake; variants affect dopamine availability.
Details
🛡️

GABA Receptors

Alcohol strongly affects GABA signaling. GABA is the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter, and alcohol enhances its effects.

GABRA2 GABRB1
GABRA2 — GABA-A Receptor Alpha-2
Strongly associated with alcohol dependence in genetic studies.
GABRB1 — GABA-A Receptor Beta-1
Contributes to sedative and anxiolytic effects of alcohol.
Details
🔥

Glutamate / NMDA System

Important for craving and withdrawal. Glutamate is the brain's primary excitatory neurotransmitter, counterbalancing GABA.

GRIN2B
GRIN2B — NMDA Receptor Subunit 2B
Involved in alcohol withdrawal severity and craving intensity.
Details
🧪

Alcohol Metabolism

Determines how alcohol is processed in the body. Variants in these enzymes significantly affect alcohol tolerance and risk.

ADH1B ALDH2
ADH1B — Alcohol Dehydrogenase 1B
Converts ethanol to acetaldehyde; fast-acting variant is protective.
ALDH2 — Aldehyde Dehydrogenase 2
Clears toxic acetaldehyde; deficiency causes alcohol flush reaction.
Details
🧠

Stress & Impulse Control

Linked to cravings and behavioral control. These genes influence how the brain handles stress, pain, and impulse regulation.

OPRM1 COMT MAOA
OPRM1 — Mu-Opioid Receptor
Target of naltrexone; mediates alcohol's pleasurable effects.
COMT — Catechol-O-Methyltransferase
Degrades dopamine; variants affect stress response and impulsivity.
MAOA — Monoamine Oxidase A
Metabolizes serotonin and dopamine; linked to aggression and risk-taking.
Details
